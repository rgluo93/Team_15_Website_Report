# Project Background

Less than 50% of Grade 4 children in Kenya are learning at the expected level (Usawa Agenda, 2023). One of the root causes is inconsistent teaching quality, which is a key determinant in students’ learning outcomes (Brookings Institution, 2022). However, teachers often lack access to adequate professional development opportunities. UNICEF (2015) revealed that most teachers in Kenya lack curriculum support and content mastery. Kenyan educators often struggle to track their progress and understand mistakes. In addition, some parts of Kenya also suffer from limited access to technology, further inhibiting educators from providing high-quality education.

The online coaching platform, LeadNow by Dignitas, is used by school leaders, teachers and education officers (Kenyan government officials responsible for overseeing school education) all across Kenya. While it was designed to provide remote coaching for teachers, its effectiveness has been hindered due to low engagement from teachers, limited accessibility and unhelpful feedback. With these problems in mind, we developed features of our project to each address a specific requirement, including personalised feedback, progress tracking, AI-driven support, multilingual access, and integrated communication tools empowering teachers to learn, reflect, and grow effectively.
