### Weeks 1–2: Gathering Requirements
<!--truncate-->
The journey began with a focused effort on understanding the scale and impact of LeadNow, and how exactly AI could meaningfully support educator development. During these first few weeks we prioritised requirements gathering and technical research to align on product and engineering goals.

On 27/10 we had our first official meeting with the Dignitas Team, facilitated by Tech to the Rescue and Prof. Sheena Visram. We learnt about the background of LeadNow, being based in Kenya, serving schools in rural areas and having a userbase of 5000+ teachers. The Dignitas team gave us a brief demo of the online learning platform, outlining the existing features and shortcomings. We learnt about the challenges that they face, including outdated phones, limited internet speed and lack of digital literacy. Based on this information, we clarified the objectives of our project and started to craft a list of detailed requirements.



Key activities in this phase included:

- Requirements gathering with stakeholders to clarify goals and success criteria.
- Technical research to evaluate RAG, LangChain, LangGraph, and suitable hosting options.
- Establishing onboarding and collaboration processes so the team could work effectively across disciplines.

This period created the blueprint for both product and engineering decisions that followed, ensuring the team shared a common vision before development started.
