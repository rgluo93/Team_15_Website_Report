Weeks 17-18: 09/03 - 22/03

We focused on scaling, performance tuning, and comprehensive testing to resolve deployment and integration edge cases before handover.

<!--truncate-->

Towards the end of the project, we continued with extensive testing, writing tests for utils, middleware, translation and speech-to-text and more. We also did meticulous manual testing of our features, trying out all the possible use cases, uncovering and promptly fixing bugs, such as with SQL generation. One notable bug was the WhatsApp chatbot sending multiple duplicate responses to one message, which was found to be caused by Meta retrying requests after our LangGraph agent took too long to respond. We then fixed the issue by scheduling processing as a background test and making use of Redis to manage message ID and state. As our features were mostly done, we worked on polishing the READMEs, making sure everything was document clearly and accurately. We also experimented with deployment using Kubernetes, but quickly shut it down due to being too expensive. 

While cleaning up, we also maintained contact with the Dignitas team, doing a final demo of the features and completing a handover meeting. We received feedback on our project shortly afterwards and thus completed the user acceptance testing.
